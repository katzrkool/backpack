Frequently Asked Questions

What is this?

This service is a convenient alternative to myBackpack that allows New School students to check their grades more easily, and get more information about their classes.

How does it work?

Once you enter your credentials on the Login Page, the service goes to myBackpack and asks politely for your grades. MyBackpack, thinking it's you, hands them over, and the service then goes to process them. It then formats it in a way pleasing to the eye. It then sends you the result!

TLDR: It gets your grades normally and formats them prettily.

Wait, are you stealing my data?

NO! The service don't store any personal data! I couldn't see your grades or credentials even if I wanted to. If you don't believe me, the code is available on Github for you to personally inspect.
The only thing stored is your IP, user agent, links visited, and time. While that may sound spooky, I can't personally identify you through that info, and you're sending that information to every site you visit anyways.
Also, occasionally, if an error pops up, some data is sent to an error management system called Sentry. The only data sent is non personally identifying information, and is completely anonymous. This data is used for bug fixing only, and is not seen any third parties.

Why should I use your thing instead myBackpack? Isn't their system the official copy?

Have you ever looked at myBackpack? All jokes aside, myBackpack can be really hard to understand, and, confusing to navigate. "My thing" is as simple can be. Type in credentials, get grades. Plus, this service provides some nifty information myBackpack doesn't provide, like how many points you can lose before dropping a letter.
And, this service grabs the exact number from myBackpack. The big grade number you see on my service, is the same thing you'll see when you log into myBackpack

What's this "you can afford to lose this many points before dropping a letter grade?"

When calculating your totals, the service looks at your assignments and calculates how many points you can lose, before dropping to the next letter grade down. For example, lets say I have an A, and I can afford to lose 20 points before hitting a B. In that class, if I'm assigned a 15 point assignment, I could technically not do it and still have an A. Of course, if you do drop a letter grade because of some anomaly, or the data changes and you skip an assignment based on previous data, or anything like that, don't come after me, I'm not responsible. This doesn't work if the class is weighted

Your analytics are wrong/The points in assignments don't add up

YEahh, so apparently myBackpack has weighted grades for some classes, but won't tell me (or you) what the weights are, so the analytics only work if there are no weights in the class.

Hey, your thing is missing assignments

That's not a question, but unfortunately, when the service goes to get your grades, myBackpack only gives back grades from whatever mode you had it in last. For example, last time you were in myBackpack, if you changed it to "show me quarter 1 grades", it will show my thing quarter 1 grades, despite my thing asking it specifically for all grades.
Easiest way to fix it is to log into myBackpack, go to Daily Assignments, click the graded tab, and change the bar at the top that says "x of 11 marking periods selected" to All. Make sure it refreshes correctly, and that all your grades are there, and the service should work properly again.

How do you deal with missing assignments?

So, myBackpack doesn't total them into your grade total, so I don't either.

Does this show the most recent quarter or all time?

Currently, it shows all time grades, since, last I heard, grades are being totalled by semester this year. If I'm wrong, please email me at lucas@lkellar.org

What happens when Semester 2 rolls around? Will it still show all time grades?

So, what's going to happen is that the big number grade will show your current grade (or whatever myBackpack deems your current grade (I've been told it should be the current semester only)), and the assignments will be seperated by Semester 1 and 2
